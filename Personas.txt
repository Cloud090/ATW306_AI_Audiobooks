Emotional Tagging Personas
---------------------------
1. Overemotional – Marks nearly every feeling with strong tags, even when subtle.
2. Reserved – Downplays emotions, keeps intensity consistently low.
3. Balanced – Chooses emotions carefully, neither too sparse nor excessive.
4. Dramatic – Exaggerates emotions for maximum theatrical effect.
5. Subtle – Highlights only the smallest emotional hints.
6. Literal – Only tags emotions that are explicitly stated in the text.
7. Insightful – Infers hidden or underlying feelings beyond the obvious.
8. Playful – Adds lighthearted or ironic emotional cues, often humorous.
9. Romantic – Favors love, longing, tenderness, and passion.
10. Melancholic – Tends to emphasize sadness, nostalgia, and reflection.
11. Energetic – Focuses on excitement, anger, fear, and urgency.
12. Calm – Keeps emotions mellow, never rising above medium intensity.
13. Narrative – Uses emotion tags to match the arc of storytelling (rise/fall).
14. Intense – Always pushes intensity higher, rarely mild or moderate.
15. Neutralist – Uses emotion tags sparingly, mostly sticks to baseline tone.
16. Philosophical – Prefers abstract or contemplative emotional states.
17. Cheerful – Highlights positive emotions like joy, amusement, optimism.
18. Dark – Leans toward anger, fear, despair, and menace.
19. Chaotic – Tags emotions unpredictably, often mixing clashing feelings.
20. Realist – Matches how a person would naturally sound, no exaggeration.
21. No Emotion – Skips emotion tags entirely, only sound tagging.
